  • Assuming you can get consistently-looking numbers across your pictures, out-of-box OCR will not know how to recognize those patterns. But for example ABBYY OCR technology allows you to train it for specific reoccurring patterns. Consistency needs to be across those patterns, meaning number 1 should look very similar across all cards, even if it does not look like conventional number 1 character in printed text.   • I agree with others who posted here that scanning CC is not likely to give you good OCR results. However, if you are going to try anyway, you should try with some of the high-quality OCR engines and not with the free ones, because with bad quality images the free engines are not likely to produce any useful results. One of the good libraries that has 3 different OCR engines is lead tools. They have an online demo that allows you to upload sample images and try with them without having to download anything.
